South Indian cuisine includes the cuisines of the five southern states of India—Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, Kerala, Tamil Nadu and Telangana—and the union territories of Lakshadweep, Pondicherry, and the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.There are typically vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes for all five states. Central India
This is the food of central India

Cuisine of Central Zone of India consists of both sweet and salty dishes. In some regions people take wheat and meat as their staple diet, but in other regions rice and fish is more common. Indore, the capital of Madhya Pradesh, offers the best of of the food of this zone and is quite popular amongst foodies. Some beverages as Thandai with milk and nuts, Kababs: their are types of kababs present here such as Shami Kabab, And sweets such as Peda which is mouth watering food known as mithai in hindi.

Clothing in India

  • Clothing

    Clothing in India is dependent upon the different climate, ethnicity, culture and traditions of the people of each region of Inida. Male and female clothing has evolved from simple garments like lungi and sari etc. to cover the body into elaborate costumes not only used in daily wears but as well as in rituals and performances. Traditional clothing for female: Clothing for women in north and east are saris worn with cholis; a long skirt called lehenga worn with choli and a dupatta; or salwar kameez suits. While South Indian women wear sari and children wear pattu langa. Sari is known with different names in different places. Mekhela Sador is the traditional Assamese dress worn by women. Rignai is the traditional dress of Tripuri women. Salwar Kameez is a generic description of the lower garment incorporating the Punjabi salwar. Lehenga Choli or Ghagra choli is the traditional clothing of women in Rajasthan and Gujrat. Traditional clothing of male: For men traditional clothes are the Sherwani, Lungi, Kurta, Jama, Shalwar Kameez and Dhoti or Pajama. Vetti, Dhotis are one of the most common male dresses found in India.Panche or Lungi is another traditional garment of India. Achkan is a small jacket that usually sports exposed buttons through the length of the jacket. Headgear as the Indian turban or the pagri is worn in many regions of the country. Other types of headgears such as Gandhi cap or Taqiyah are worn by different comunities within the country.
